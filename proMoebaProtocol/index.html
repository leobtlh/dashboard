Multi-Slice Architecture

The HPIV standard introduces a capital hierarchy to absorb shocks without liquidating investors.

"First Loss" Capital

Segmentation via Junior Tranche (ERC-20) and Senior Tranche (ERC-4626). The insurer (Sponsor) commits their own capital first.

Risk Segmentation

The protocol requires the insurer to deposit a First Loss Capital (Junior Tranche). This safety buffer is sacrificed first in the event of a claim.

Investors (LPs) mostly position themselves on the Senior Tranche. Their liquidity is only exposed after the total exhaustion of the insurer's capital and the Junior Tranche.

Waterfall Mechanism

In the event of a catastrophe, the triggerCatastrophe function executes a sequential absorption of losses.

"Soft Default" Simulation

Whitepaper Example (Vault $40M, Claim $8M):

  • 1. Insurer: Loses their $4M First Loss (100% loss).
  • 2. Buffer: The Premium Reserve absorbs the remainder.
  • 3. Junior Tranche: Absorbs the remaining $4M (80% loss).
  • 4. Senior Tranche: Intact (0% loss).

Result: The Senior tranche is protected by the sacrifice of lower tranches.

Yield Splitting

Asymmetric yield distribution.

Yield Leverage

Senior: Receives 70% of the base APR in exchange for safety (Safety First).

Junior: Captures leverage on the remaining premiums (30% of Senior yield redirected), mechanically boosting its APR (Yield Seekers).

Financial Engineering

On-chain mathematical security and data integrity via Oracles.

Oracles & Security

Automated triggering by chain-agnostic oracles and protection against ERC-4626 attack vectors.

Technical Integrity

The Vault integrates advanced defense mechanisms:

  1. Anti-Inflation Attack: Minting of "Dead Shares" at initialization to lock the exchange rate and prevent donation attacks.
  2. Certified Oracles: Integration of data streams (USGS, NOAA) for objective claim triggering.

Over-Collateralization

The contract mathematically verifies TotalAssets >= Capacity before any deposit. The risk is always "Fully Funded" to eliminate counterparty risk.

Premium Reserve

The premiumReserve is ring-fenced and isolated from risk capital.

Guaranteed Yield

The premium paid by the insurer is stored in a distinct state variable. It serves as a secondary buffer in the Waterfall (after insurer capital) but never constitutes the primary risk collateral.

At maturity, this reserve is the sole source of yield distributed to Senior and Junior investors.

Swiss Regulatory Framework

Designed according to the revised ISA requirements and the DLT/TRD Law.

SPV Vehicle (Art. 30e ISA)

Legal structure of "Insurance Special Purpose Vehicle" (ISPV). Risk transformer fully funded by the capital market.

Revised ISA Compliance

The protocol respects the "Fully Funded" requirement (Art. 111d ISO): the ISPV holds assets covering the maximum exposure at all times.

Use of exemptions for "Professional Policyholders" (Art. 30a ISA) to optimize solvency and organizational fund requirements.

DLT Law (Art. 973d CO)

HPIV tokens are qualified as Ledger-based Securities (Droits-Valeurs Inscrits).

Legal Certainty

Under the TRD/DLT Law, the blockchain acts as the official registry of ownership. Token transfer is legally equivalent to the transfer of the claim, offering legal robustness to institutional investors without a central custodian.

AMLA & FinSA Compliance

"Permissioned DeFi" architecture with Whitelisting (KYB/KYC) via the Factory.

Anti-Money Laundering Framework

The ISPV acts as a financial intermediary (Art. 2 AMLA). The HPIVFactory Smart Contract manages a registry of whitelisted insurers.

Targeting Qualified Investors allows for prospectus exemption (FinSA), aligning the protocol with institutional standards.
